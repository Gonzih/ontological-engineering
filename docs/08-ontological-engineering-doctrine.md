# Ontological Engineering Doctrine

This document states the repository's core doctrine. It converts the academic synthesis into a sharper position: what ontological engineering is, what it rejects, what it borrows, and what it uniquely organizes.

## Core Claim

Ontological engineering is the discipline of designing the explicit conceptual, epistemic, temporal, and operational structure that lets an AI-native company remember, reason, decide, generate, and revise coherently.

This is not the same as ontology engineering, knowledge management, knowledge graphs, requirements engineering, or prompt engineering. It uses pieces of all of them, but the unit of design is different.

The unit of design is company cognition.

## What We Borrow

From knowledge engineering, we borrow elicitation, structuring, formalization, and operationalization.

From knowledge representation and reasoning, we borrow the idea that intelligent behavior depends on what an agent can know and reason over.

From ontology engineering, we borrow explicit conceptualization, ontology lifecycle, reuse, alignment, and evolution.

From knowledge graphs, we borrow entity-relationship infrastructure, identity, context, and graph traversal.

From provenance standards, we borrow source, derivation, activity, agent, and transformation semantics.

From belief revision, we borrow rational change operations: expansion, contraction, revision, merging, and entrenchment.

From organizational memory, we borrow the idea that companies preserve and use past interpretations, decisions, and experience.

From design rationale and IBIS, we borrow structured questions, positions, arguments, alternatives, and decision reasoning.

From requirements engineering, we borrow goal modeling, assumptions, constraints, agent responsibility, operationalization, traceability, and validation.

## What We Reject

We reject treating the company knowledge base as a document archive.

We reject treating semantic search as sufficient for organizational intelligence.

We reject treating generated agent output as knowledge by default.

We reject specifications that are only prompts, tickets, or implementation instructions.

We reject current-state memory that overwrites the evolution of thinking.

We reject knowledge graphs that model entities but not epistemic status, provenance, lifecycle, or actionability.

We reject requirements that cannot be traced back to claims, evidence, assumptions, and decisions.

We reject agent autonomy without explicit permission boundaries and promotion rules.

## The Eight Primitives

Ontological engineering organizes company cognition around eight primitive dimensions.

## 1. Entity

What kind of thing is this?

The system must distinguish concepts, claims, evidence, beliefs, assumptions, decisions, rationales, specifications, artifacts, feedback, and agent outputs. Without type, agents cannot know how to treat an object.

## 2. Meaning

What does this thing mean?

Meaning includes definition, boundary conditions, examples, counterexamples, scope, aliases, and relationships to other concepts. Meaning prevents teams and agents from using the same term in incompatible ways.

## 3. Evidence

Why should we believe this?

Evidence includes source, observation, interpretation, quality, limitations, and links to claims. Evidence prevents claims from floating free of justification.

## 4. Status

What is this allowed to do?

Status includes raw, proposed, accepted, disputed, rejected, superseded, deprecated, and historical. Status turns epistemology into permissions. A proposed claim can be explored. An accepted belief can inform specifications. A deprecated belief can explain history but should not guide new work.

## 5. Time

When was this true, known, believed, revised, or superseded?

Time includes observed_at, created_at, valid_from, valid_until, reviewed_at, superseded_at, and implementation dates. Time lets the company reconstruct past belief states and detect software built on stale assumptions.

## 6. Relationship

What does this depend on, support, challenge, constrain, generate, or implement?

Relationships turn isolated records into an intelligence graph. The important relationships are typed and directional: supports, contradicts, depends_on, supersedes, derived_from, constrains, implements, generates, reviewed_by, and owned_by.

## 7. Agency

Who or what can act on this?

Agency includes human roles, agent roles, permissions, tool access, memory access, review requirements, and escalation conditions. Agency prevents accidental knowledge corruption and unsafe software generation.

## 8. Consequence

What changes if this changes?

Consequence links knowledge to downstream decisions, requirements, specifications, prompts, tests, code, workflows, and product behavior. This is where ontological engineering becomes operational. A belief change should trigger dependency review.

## The Company Cognition Loop

The doctrine can be expressed as a loop:

1. Reality produces signals.
2. Humans and agents capture sources.
3. Sources yield evidence.
4. Evidence supports or challenges claims.
5. Claims become assumptions, hypotheses, beliefs, or disagreements.
6. Beliefs and assumptions shape decisions.
7. Decisions and rationale shape specifications.
8. Specifications generate artifacts.
9. Artifacts produce feedback.
10. Feedback revises the ontology, epistemology, and specifications.

This loop is the spine of ontological engineering.

## Why This Is Juicy

The idea is strong because it sits at the intersection of several pressures that are all increasing:

- companies are generating more artifacts than they can govern
- AI agents need structured memory, not just context windows
- software generation makes specification quality more important
- organizational memory becomes more fragile as information velocity rises
- semantic search is useful but insufficient for decision traceability
- agent outputs need provenance and promotion rules
- product systems increasingly encode old assumptions invisibly

The opportunity is to build the discipline and tooling that makes an AI-native company more coherent as it grows.

## Confidence Position

The concept is not 100 percent proven as a product category yet. That requires implementation and user pressure. But as a research and design thesis, confidence is now high because:

- the primitives map to established academic traditions
- the integration problem is real and under-solved
- the AI-native company context changes the urgency
- the lens creates a coherent path from philosophy to schema to agents to software
- the repository now has a clear doctrine, not just descriptive essays

The next confidence jump will come from a worked implementation: raw organizational input becoming structured knowledge, then a specification, then generated software, then feedback that revises the knowledge base.
